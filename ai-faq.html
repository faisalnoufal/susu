General Kerala Travel Questions

What is the best time to visit Kerala?

The best time to visit Kerala is between October and March when the weather is pleasant and ideal for all activities. The monsoon season (June to September) offers lush green landscapes but some activities may be limited. April and May can be hot and humid but are good for hill station visits like Munnar.

Topics: Weather, Seasons, Travel Planning

How many days are ideal for a Kerala trip?

We recommend 7-10 days for a comprehensive Kerala experience. This allows you to explore the major destinations like Munnar (2 days), Thekkady (2 days), Alleppey houseboat (1-2 days), and Kovalam/Varkala beaches (2 days). For a more relaxed pace or to include more destinations, 12-14 days would be ideal.

Topics: Itinerary, Trip Duration, Travel Planning

What are the must-visit places in Kerala?

The must-visit destinations in Kerala include:

  • Munnar - Famous for its tea plantations and cool climate
  • Alleppey (Alappuzha) - Known for backwaters and houseboat cruises
  • Thekkady - Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Kovalam - Popular beach destination
  • Wayanad - Hill station with wildlife and trekking opportunities
  • Fort Kochi - Historical area with colonial architecture
  • Varkala - Beach with dramatic cliffs and relaxed atmosphere
Topics: Destinations, Attractions, Popular Places

How long should I book a houseboat for?

We recommend booking a houseboat for at least one night (24 hours) to fully experience the backwaters. This typically involves boarding around noon, cruising until evening, anchoring for the night, and continuing the journey the next morning until checkout around 9-10 AM. For a more comprehensive backwater experience, a 2-night stay allows you to explore more remote areas and enjoy a more relaxed pace.

Accommodation and Transportation

What types of accommodation are available in Kerala?

Kerala offers a wide range of accommodation options:

  • Luxury Resorts - 5-star properties with all amenities
  • Boutique Hotels - Unique, smaller properties with personalized service
  • Houseboats - Traditional rice barges converted into floating hotels
  • Homestays - Stay with local families for an authentic experience
  • Ayurvedic Retreats - Specialized accommodations focused on wellness
  • Eco Resorts - Sustainable properties in natural settings
  • Beach Resorts - Properties along Kerala's coastline
  • Plantation Stays - Accommodations on tea, coffee, or spice plantations
Topics: Accommodation, Hotels, Homestays, Resorts

What is the best way to get around in Kerala?

The best ways to travel around Kerala include:

  • Private Car with Driver - Most convenient and flexible option, especially for multi-destination trips
  • Public Buses - Economical but can be crowded and time-consuming
  • Trains - Good for longer distances between major cities
  • Ferries - In backwater regions, local ferries are an authentic way to travel
  • Taxis and Auto Rickshaws - Good for short distances within cities

For most tourists, we recommend booking a package with a private vehicle and driver for the most comfortable and efficient travel experience.

Topics: Transportation, Travel Methods, Getting Around

Food and Culture

What are the must-try foods in Kerala?

Kerala cuisine is diverse and flavorful. Must-try dishes include:

  • Kerala Sadya - Traditional feast served on banana leaf
  • Appam with Stew - Lacy rice pancakes with coconut milk stew
  • Kerala Parotta with Beef Curry - Layered flatbread with spicy beef
  • Karimeen Pollichathu - Pearl spot fish marinated and grilled in banana leaf
  • Puttu and Kadala Curry - Steamed rice cake with black chickpea curry
  • Thalassery Biryani - Unique Kerala-style fragrant rice dish
  • Pazham Pori - Banana fritters, a popular snack
  • Kerala Prawn Curry - Prawns in coconut milk and spices
Topics: Food, Cuisine, Local Dishes, Culinary Experience

What cultural experiences should I not miss in Kerala?

Kerala offers rich cultural experiences that you shouldn't miss:

  • Kathakali Performance - Traditional dance drama with elaborate costumes and makeup
  • Kalaripayattu Show - Ancient martial art demonstration
  • Temple Festivals - Colorful processions with elephants and traditional music
  • Theyyam Ritual - Sacred dance ritual in North Kerala
  • Cooking Class - Learn to prepare authentic Kerala dishes
  • Spice Plantation Tour - Understand the importance of spices in Kerala's history
  • Village Life Experience - Visit rural areas to see traditional crafts and lifestyle
  • Ayurvedic Treatment - Experience traditional wellness therapies
Topics: Culture, Arts, Traditions, Experiences

Practical Travel Information

What is the average cost of a Kerala trip?

The cost of a Kerala trip varies based on your travel style:

  • Budget Travel: ₹2,500-4,000 per person per day (including basic accommodation, local transport, and meals)
  • Mid-range: ₹5,000-8,000 per person per day (including good hotels, private transport, and quality dining)
  • Luxury: ₹10,000+ per person per day (including premium resorts, exclusive experiences, and fine dining)

A 7-day package typically ranges from ₹20,000 (budget) to ₹70,000+ (luxury) per person, excluding flights. Houseboat stays generally cost between ₹8,000 (basic) to ₹25,000+ (premium) per night for a two-bedroom boat.

Topics: Budget, Costs, Expenses, Planning

Is Kerala safe for tourists?

Yes, Kerala is considered one of the safest destinations in India for tourists. The state has a high literacy rate, well-developed tourism infrastructure, and is known for its friendly locals. As with any destination, standard travel precautions apply:

  • Keep valuables secure and be aware of your surroundings
  • Use registered transportation services
  • Follow local customs and dress modestly, especially at religious sites
  • Stay hydrated and be cautious with street food
  • Purchase travel insurance that covers medical emergencies

Kerala also has a special tourism police force dedicated to assisting visitors and ensuring their safety.
